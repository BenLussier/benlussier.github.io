ABOUT

Raised by an engineer father, I often played with LEGOs, assembled new appliances, and helped with household projects. My artistic interests started at a young age and over the years has migrated from physical to digital mediums. My first engineering job was an intern at Hamilton Sundstrand (Now UTC Aerospace) during the summer of 2008. The next summer, I interned at the well-known firearms manufacturer, Smith & Wesson (S&W), and continued my internship while I finished my senior year. In May 2010, I received my undergraduate degree in industrial engineering from Western New England University. Shortly after, I was hired full-time by S&W as a manufacturing engineer. My work consisted of a wide range of tasks including process design & improvement, troubleshooting, work instruction creation, fixture design, software development, product improvement, laser engraving, barcode tracking, and many more. I received my masters in mechanical engineering, with a concentration in mechatronics, from Western New England University in 2013 while working full-time. In 2015, I was promoted to a special engineer position where I managed and implemented capital projects related to bringing new technology to the plant including the technical aspects of the projects. Between 2012 and 2017, I taught a software engineering class at my alma mater. In August 2017, I moved to Utah to enjoy the great outdoors and start my consulting business. In my free time, I enjoy hiking, camping, shooting, volleyball, skydiving, speedflying, and anything outdoors.
