Mechatronics systems design is a broad field of science that combines many fields of engineering into one. When referring to the manufacturing environment, Mechatronics is more commonly known as Industrial Automation. Mechatronic systems may incorporate mechanical engineering, electronics, software, systems engineering, robotics, and control engineering. However, as technology advances and systems become more complex, the field of mechatronics continues to expand. To learn more about mechatronics, visit the Wikipedia page here.

FEATURES

Software.

The brains of any system. Custom built software running on a computer or PLC provides logic flow and user interfaces tailored to your exact requirements.

Databases.

Data. Data. Data. Leverage powerful data platforms like SQL, SAP, Oracle, and many others.

I/O.

Utilize sensors to collect data and provide system feedback. Force, temperature, size, presence, color, machine vision. You name it.

DAQ.

Data Acquisition (DAQ) boards create a seamless product by bridging the gap between sensor inputs/outputs and software/databases.

IoT.

(Internet of Things) Connect systems to a larger network of devices to make use of their full potential.

Mechanical.

Incorporate mechanical, electromechanical, hydraulic, or pneumatic components.

Documentation.

Receive accurate, thorough documentation to facilitate user operation, preventative maintenance, and troubleshooting.
